And we develop in ephemeral compartmentalized virtual machines, and we deploy to ephemeral compartmentalized virtual machines (or our own open source hardware, for hardware compartmentalization when possible, and necessary); not containers.

Unlike many other developers and companies who are racing to the finish line, fixing bugs as they see them, we assume nothing will work, and go beyond test driven development, fuzzing our software, and validating every input into any applicaiton.

We use a security in depth approach, for example, we keep all data encrypted when at rest, and never store more information than necessary. These are very different strategies than the norms. We use as little javascript as possible, do whitelist based filtering for XSS, and SQLi.

When other developers say, "well, an attack would never occur like this, or from this angle"; we assume it will and defend against it.

Even in the rare event, our database is breached, very little of the data would be usable because of our policy of encrypting all data at rest, and with actually secure cryptography algorithms.

We will teach you how to use cryptography, not just for sending messages containing secrets but the far more important use-case for encryption: verifying our identity, and the validity of our messages and documents. Every document, and every message will be signed and verifable. So you can absolutely be sure it came from us.

We do not typically provide development services, we are primarily a security research workers cooperative.

Our primary source of income is finding exploits existing in enterprise level software, that buildings of engineers who are supposedly the best miss; and we do it well.

For example, one of our founding members, of the Hackwave Laboratories workers cooperative, hacked a Bitcoin ATM in under 5 minutes of first interacting with it, resulting it giving double payouts. Responsible disclosure company resulted in a bug bounty reward of 20,000 USD.

We only deviate from our primary role as security consultancy for special cases we feel are worth investing the time into.
