The forums functioning as both as a knowledge-base, and a way to drive traffic, and market your law firm through a internet presence that offers more than a business-card, are the foundation.

In the design specification for the web application, we mentioned using OAuth2 account system; so that potential users with accounts on Google, Facebook, Twitter, and others, can use often use their existing session to create an account and login with a few clicks.

We can request some basic information from these services for building our accounts, like their name, email, and we could have more information added later by the user. While this is initially used to lower the barrier of entry to posting, making it simple as possible, avoiding requiring another account creation, confirming an email; we use their existing accounts to verify them faster, and easier.

But this has added benefit for the purposes of using these forum accounts as potential client accounts.

An important benefit from OAuth2 account systems besides the low-barrier of enry, ensures the accounts are not regular throw-aways. When verifying a user through OAuth2 accounts already created on Google, Facebook, and Twiter; they are already vettted, and will most likely be legimtiate users, with real emails already confirmed by these services. These ensure that many of these accounts will be ready to use as customer accounts in a payment system too.

Customer Relationship Management (CRM)

Another benefit of using a full web application over the static web page is the account system can easily be turned into a customer relationship management (CRM) system.

"Customer relationship management (CRM) is an approach to managing a company's interaction with current and potential customers. It uses data analysis about customers' history with a company to improve business relationships with customers, specifically focusing on customer retention and ultimately driving sales growth."
Wikipedia CRM Article

Since these accounts are already vetted, most of them will be reliable for a client management system. And so we can also support accepting payments through the web application, and offer services directly through the web application. This could be a store-like presentation where potential clients can "purchase" services, either a down-payment to start the client relationship, with further invocing entered into the system manually, and allowing the customers to pay over time, this even makes possible payment plans.

But this could be a full client management system tracking all clients, at every stage of the process, and storing all relevant information, and handling payments.

Start from tracking potential client leads, tracking all active clients and have a system to track and monitor the stage of their cases, and store information about previous clients after scrubbing all sensitive data.

Hackwave Laboratories always takes a security first approach to our design, and we would avoid storing any unncessary personal information about clients, keep all data encrypted when at rest, and the ssystem would run in a secure environment we setup that would make database leaks as unlikely as possible, and if ever they occur, leaking as little information as possible, by keeping all data encrypted unless the account is acively being used which is dramaticlaly different than how many other web applications are built.

Using a simple service like Stripe Payments you can take credit cards for roughly 3% fees, which overall are reasonable, and their system is very easy to intergrate and use. But this service only works within the US. If your service expands into latin america, we can switch to something like Braintree Payments.

We recommend adding Venmo Payments, a service that is growing in popularity,and so it would be worth adding support, which would be very easy to do. And we can add support for the older payment services like Paypal. Eventually we can even add Bitcoin.

Accepting cryptocurrency?

However, for the case of Bitcoin, you will find the best solution will be to simply just state on the site you accept Bitcoin. Then if anyone actually decides to pay in Bitcoin, you can just handle it manually. It will likely be rare, and can do it by a case-by-case basis; because the entire point is there is no need for middlemen, and peope using it will appreciate that. If you do end getting regular business in Bitcoin, we can then setup a system for accepting it; its very easy to do and can be done so that its direct, and there are zero fees for that payment method.
